A little more context, in my own words.

I didn’t start in software. My first qualification was a Diploma in Electrical and Electronic Engineering at PSG College of Technology. I got curious about the systems connecting those circuits and moved into Computer Science at Anna University Regional Campus Coimbatore, graduating with an 8.57 CGPA.

That hardware-first path still shapes how I think. A web app, a firmware binary, and an industrial protocol aren’t three separate disciplines to me — they’re stacks of abstractions, and every abstraction leaks somewhere. That’s where vulnerabilities live, and that’s where I enjoy looking.

How I got here2021 – present

I started professionally as a security analyst intern at Strongbox IT, testing web and mobile applications for BFSI and healthcare clients and contributing to a Flask-based web application firewall. It taught me that good security work is rigour plus communication, and that the boring parts are not optional.

At Aujas Cybersecurity (now NuSummit) I was deployed to Sony India Software Center for nearly three years. I led offensive assessments across web, mobile, and cross-platform OTT — tvOS, webOS, TizenOS — surfacing payment bypasses, DRM misconfigurations, business-logic issues, and memory corruption. I built custom fuzzing tooling, automated over 70% of repetitive assessments in Python and Bash, and was promoted to Senior Consultant along the way.

At Festo the stakes shifted. Industrial controls, Profinet and Modbus, firmware and edge controllers — systems with physical consequences. I’ve driven more than a dozen critical and high-severity findings to remediation, facilitated TARA across product lifecycles aligned to upcoming CRA compliance, and helped grow the product security team from four engineers to ten.

What I care aboutin the work

Reports are worthless without fixes. Every finding I write comes with remediation guidance that engineering can actually ship. I work with development teams, not against them — the goal is a closed issue, not a scary PDF.

Scanners miss the interesting bugs. I automate the repetitive so I can spend real attention on business-logic flaws, memory corruption in custom protocols, and the kind of issues that require an actual attacker’s mindset.

Teaching is part of the job. I’ve mentored engineers from non-security backgrounds, delivered training on fuzzing and reverse engineering, and release open-source tooling when it’s useful. Security that only lives in one person’s head is a liability.

Disclose responsibly. Executives need clarity, engineers need reproduction steps, and the public needs timely coordinated disclosure. CVE-2022-2912 and the Oracle Hall of Fame entry came from following that playbook, not skipping it.
